About Me

Hey there! I'm a Computer Science and Applied Mathematics student at UC Berkeley with a concentration in Economics. You could say I'm the kind of person who finds beauty in both elegant code and elegant equations — and yes, I do get excited about mathematical proofs!

Currently, I'm diving deep into the fascinating world of expander graphs and group theory through Berkeley's Mathematics DRP. It's like solving puzzles with infinite dimensions, and I'm here for it! As I've recently discovered in this process, front-end development is definitely not my forte (as you might have noticed), but I'm always up for learning new things.

When I'm not buried in math theorems or debugging code, you'll find me performing with Cal Bhangra dance team (yes, I can debug AND dance!) or geeking out about startups as a Sutardja Dai Center Innovation Fellow. I believe in the power of interdisciplinary thinking — because sometimes the best solutions come from unexpected connections.
